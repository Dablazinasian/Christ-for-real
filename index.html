Chapter 2

I know that we're all very busy. For some of y'all, this isn't your only book you're reading consistently. So I want to encourage y'all to be highlighting or jotting down interesting points Price makes or verses that really tied everything together for you.

Two passages that could be read alongside this chapter: - Romans 7:7-25 OR - Ephesians 4:17-32
While reading Chapter 2, you might notice that it's very similar to chapter 1. Here are a few distinctions to focus on: - Depth of Alienation: why does our failure exist - Glory of God: ultimate standard - Nothing is impossible: hopeful perspective

As you read the chapter, consider some ways that you can reflect God's character.

A couple of examples: - God's unseen presence in the mundane: God is always with us, even in routine tasks. Reflect His character by quietly serving a family member without expecting any recognition. - God’s patience and purposefulness: God’s words are powerful yet restrained, showing patience and purpose. Reflect His character by choosing silence and listening deeply rather than trying to fix or control situations with words. - God's unshakeable peace: God remains steady and unflustered, no matter the circumstances. Reflect His character by anchoring yourself in faith, especially during stressful or uncertain times. - God's heart for justice and mercy: God seeks justice but tempers it with compassion and understanding. Reflect His character by standing up for what is right in a way that also extends empathy and mercy. - God's steadfast joy: God’s joy transcends circumstances, flowing from His very nature. Reflect His character by finding and expressing gratitude and joy, even in difficult seasons.
